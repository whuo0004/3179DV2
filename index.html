Comparing The Best:

Times Higher Education (THE) University Rankings Insights

Times Higher Education (THE) rankings are widely recognized as one of the most respected and influential global university ranking systems. These rankings assess and compare different universities around the world based on multiple criterias. These criterias are teaching, research, citations, industry income, and international outlook. Other than that, this ranking also provides information on the number of students, staff and student ratio and international student percentage. THE rankings offer valuable insights into the performance, reputation and environment of different universities, which helps in making informed decisions when choosing a university.

This visualisation aims to explore and visualises different aspects of these rankings. The main sections of this visualisation explores the rankings over time based on score, the scores given to each universities in each criteria, possible correlations between scores and student/staff informations, and details on the location of these universities. To jump to a specific section, please use the menu located on the top of the screen.

Rankings: Rank fluctuation of Top 20 University in 2023 (2013-2023)

The above chart visualises the fluctuation in rankings of the top 20 universities in 2023 over the years. These rankings are determined by many different factors that this visualisation will continue to explore.
It can be observed that most of the universities holding a high ranking has been holding on to a similarly high ranking over the years. However, some exceptions such as TsingHua University and National University of Singapore climbed the ranks over the years and successfully placed in the top 20 in 2023.

Correlations: Stats vs Different Scores (2023 - Top 50)

The above chart visualises the correlations of statistics and scores of the top 50 universities in 2023. Some observation that can be made includes universities that have more international students usually have a higher international outlook. and that universities with a low student to staff ratio usually hava a higher teaching score.

Top 50 University by Chosen Ranking and Chosen Indicator 2023

The following chart visualises the rankings and the indicator scores selected by the User. The chart is sorted based on the rankings selected where the first row will be the university ranked 1 in the selected ranking and the bar visualises the score obtained by the university in the selected indicator. By hovering over the bar, the tooltip shows the values in details.

The filters are available at the bottom of the chart.




The rankings does not necessary indicate how good a university is but is used to inform the statistics or scores in a indicator. Having the top rank in any category does not mean the university is the most suitable. Different individuals must have their ownopinion on what is the most suitable based on themselves.


Location: Number of Top 1000 Universities in different countries

The map below visualises the number of top 1000 universities in each different countries.
It can be observed that countries such as the United States of America, United Kingdom and China have the most top 1000 universities. It can also be observed that most small countries have at least a few universities in the top 1000. However, less developed countries usually have no universities ranked in the top 1000. From these observation, it can be concluded that the number of top universities in a country depends on how developed the country are. Vice versa, this map also shows which country seems to be more developed.
